Expert Knowledge

Tips & Tricks to Master Word Games

Level up your game with these proven strategies used by competitive word game players worldwide.

1

Learn High-Value Short Words

Two-letter and three-letter words like QI, ZA, OX, and JO are essential Scrabble weapons. Memorize the full list of valid 2-letter words — there are 101 of them in the official dictionary.

2

Balance Vowels and Consonants

An ideal Scrabble rack has 2–3 vowels. Too many vowels (like AAEIOU) limits your options dramatically. Exchange tiles early if your rack is unbalanced rather than making a weak play.

3

Use Prefixes and Suffixes

Adding S, ED, ING, ER, EST, UN-, RE-, or PRE- to existing board words (hooking) is one of the most powerful strategies in Scrabble.

4

Target Premium Squares

Always aim to place high-value letters (Q, Z, X, J) on Double Letter or Triple Letter score squares. A QI on a triple letter score earns 30 points from just two letters!

5

Wordle Strategy: Start Broad

Begin Wordle with words that cover common letters: CRANE, SLATE, or RAISE are statistically excellent openers. Eliminate as many letters as possible in your first two guesses.

6

Keep High-Flexibility Tiles

The letters S, E, R, and blank tiles are the most flexible in any word game. Don't waste them on low-scoring plays — save them for setting up bigger, higher-scoring moves.

What Is a Word Finder and How Does It Help You?

A word finder is a web-based application that acts as a helper or solver for word-based games and puzzles. Whether you're playing Scrabble on a Friday night, competing in Words With Friends on your commute, or agonizing over today's Wordle, a word finder tool gives you instant access to every valid word possible from your available letters.

Our word finder works by taking the letters you input and cross-referencing them against a comprehensive English dictionary. Within milliseconds, the algorithm identifies every valid word that can be formed using some or all of your letters — then sorts the results by Scrabble score so the highest-value plays appear first.

Who Uses a Word Finder?

Word finders are used by a diverse range of people:

  • Casual players who want help when they're stuck and can't see a word in their rack
  • Competitive Scrabble players who use it to study and improve their vocabulary
  • Students and teachers using it as a vocabulary-building educational tool
  • Puzzle enthusiasts solving crosswords, word jumbles, and anagram challenges
  • Non-native English speakers learning the language through word games
  • Writers and content creators looking for specific word structures or rhymes

Word Finder vs Word Unscrambler: What's the Difference?

A word finder finds all words that can be made from a subset of your letters — you don't need to use every letter. A word unscrambler finds words using all or most of your scrambled letters. Our tool does both: the main finder uses any combination of your letters, while the dedicated Unscramble tool focuses on using as many of your letters as possible.

Both are invaluable for different scenarios. Use the word finder when you have a full Scrabble rack and want maximum options. Use the unscrambler when you're given a specific set of scrambled letters and need to identify the intended word.
